# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RefForge is primarily for a solo game creator or small creative operator who collects game asset references while designing original game assets, UI, environments, materials, and production ideas.

The user works in focused research sessions: collecting source links, checking ownership and license context, classifying references, and translating observations into reusable creative principles. The interface should support repeated scanning, editing, comparison, and review rather than public browsing.

## Product Purpose

RefForge / `灵感锻造台` is a private-first game asset reference research desk.

It exists to turn scattered source links into structured reference records with clear source, license, public-safety, classification, scoring, tags, and inspiration extraction fields. Success means a user can preserve useful references, understand why they are useful, avoid copying protected expression, and move toward original asset creation with a clean trail of decisions.

The product is not a public asset gallery, download site, marketplace, or crawler. It is a working research surface for private creative thinking.

## Brand Personality

Focused, crafted, discerning.

The product should feel like a serious creative workstation: calm enough for research, dense enough for repeated use, and distinctive enough to feel like a personal forge for ideas. It can feel tactile and editorial, but it should not become decorative or theatrical.

## Anti-references

- Public asset showcase pages where thumbnails are treated as downloadable goods.
- Marketing dashboard templates with oversized heroes, empty metrics, and decorative panels.
- Neon game HUD aesthetics that overpower source review and writing.
- Generic AI-app dark dashboards with purple gradients and vague productivity language.
- Note apps that collapse all insight into undifferentiated text blocks.

## Design Principles

1. Workbench first: the first screen is always the usable research desk, not a landing page.
2. Safety before inspiration: source, license, public status, and avoid-copying guidance remain visible before creative extraction.
3. Dense but calm: card density should support comparison without turning the page into visual noise.
4. Structured thinking: observation, principle, transferable idea, original application, and avoid-copying notes are treated as first-class fields.
5. Private by default: the interface should reinforce that saved references are private unless explicitly reviewed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA for contrast, keyboard focus, form labels, and state communication.

Status must not rely on color alone. All form fields need visible labels. Destructive actions need confirmation. Motion should be minimal, state-driven, and compatible with reduced-motion preferences. Chinese is the default interface language, with English available as a clear secondary option.
